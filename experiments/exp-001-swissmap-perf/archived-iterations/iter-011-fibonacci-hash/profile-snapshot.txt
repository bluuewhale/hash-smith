## Hash function analysis
Current: Hashing.smear() = C2 * Integer.rotateLeft(hashCode * C1, 15)
  - 2 × 32-bit multiplications
  - 1 × rotateLeft (compiled to single rotate instruction)
  - Total: ~3 instructions in the multiply chain

Fibonacci alternative: (int)(((long)hashCode * 0x9e3779b97f4a7c15L) >>> 32)
  - 1 × 64-bit multiplication (single IMULQ on x86-64)
  - 1 × unsigned right shift
  - Total: ~2 instructions, single multiply latency on modern CPUs (3-4 cycles vs ~6-9 for Murmur chain)

## Hypothesis
hash() is called once per putVal before putValHashed. At 12K (L1/L2 resident),
the hash computation contributes ~1-2ns out of the 6ns per-op cost.
Reducing from 3-multiply chain to 1-multiply could yield 5-15% improvement on PutHit@12K and PutMiss@12K.
784K benchmarks are L3-latency dominated; improvement expected to be smaller.

## Distribution quality
Fibonacci hashing with 0x9e3779b97f4a7c15L (64-bit golden ratio constant) is
widely validated (used in Knuth, Rust std, etc). For Integer keys the avalanche
is sufficient for SwissTable h2 (7-bit fingerprint).
